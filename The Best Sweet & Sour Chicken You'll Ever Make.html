The Best Sweet & Sour Chicken You'll Ever Make

Are you basic like me and love simple breaded chicken with a sweet and tangy sauce?

Well have I got the recipe for you!

Perfect and simple, you can put this over rice or noodles and add veg if you'd like.

Ingredients

  • 1 Chicken Breast
  • 1 Egg
  • 1 1/4 cup (or 300mL) of Water
  • 2 tsp Salt
  • 2 tsp Garlic Powder
  • A pinch of white pepper
  • 1/4 cup (or 31 g) of Corn Starch
  • 1 1/4 cup (or 157 g)
  • 3 tbsp Veg. Oil
  • 1 tbsp Cooking Wine (optional)

Instructions

  1. Slice your chicken breast in half, slice the halves into 3 long strips, turn each strip sideways and slice it at a 45 degree angle into peices about an inch wide.
  2. Add your chicken into a bowl with 1 tsp of salt, 1 tsp of garlic powder, a dash of white peper, your optional cooking wine, 1/4 cup (or 60 mL) of water and mix well.
  3. Wrap your bowl in plastic wrap and refrigerate for 30 min-1 hr.
    4. In a mixing bowl add your egg, 1 tsp of garlic powder, a dash of white pepper, 1 tsp of salt, 1/4 cup (or 31 g) of corn starch, 1 cup (or 250 mL) of water.
  4. Start stiring gently and the beat it. After a while add 1 cup (or 125 g) of flour first mix it in, then add the other 1/4 cup and mix well. Make sure to scrape the side of the bowl.
  5. Mix in 3 tbsp of veg. oil, then add 1 tbsp of baking powder. Your batter should be thick so it will stick to your chicken.
  6. Take your bowl of chicken out of the fridge, you should see minimal water left, pour in your batter and mix well.
  7. In a pan or wok heat up a good ammount of veg. oil to 370 F (or 187 C), use a fork to put your chicken in the oil, it should look fat and puffy when it rises above the oil, make sure you continuously move around your chicken so it will have an even colour.
  8. About 2 min into frying take your chicken out of the oil, let sit for about 30 sec, then put your chicken back in the oil.
  9. cook for about 3 more minutes, or until it is as crispy as you would like.
  10. Take your chicken out of the oil and serve with your sauce.

Ingredients For Sauce:

  • 10 cups (or 2.3 L) of Water
  • 2 Oranges
  • 1 Lemon
  • 2-3 slices of Ginger
  • 1 1/3 cups (or 315 mL)Vinegar
  • 1 1/2 cups (or 355 g) of Sugar
  • 1 1/4 cups (or 300 mL) Ketchup
  • 1 tbsp of corn starch

Cooking Instructions For Sauce:

  1. Add your 10 cups of water and bring it to a boil.
  2. While the water boils, slice your oranges, lemon, and ginger.Once the water is boiling, add them in.
  3. Boil for about 30 min, or until the oranges and lemons soft and the center is almost falling out. You want to make sure you have 5-6 cups (or 1.2 L) of juice there.
  4. Drain the juice into a bowlusing a strainer that wil catch all the organges, lemons, and ginger. Use a spoon to press down on your oranges and lemons and get every last drop.
  5. To the juice, add your 1 1/2 cup (or 355 g) of sugar,1 1/3 cup (or 315 mL) of vinegar,and your 1 1/4 cup (or 300 mL) of Ketchup and mix well.(in restraunts they add 1 tsp of tomato shade to get a darker red)
  6. In your pot add 1 cup (or 236 mL of your sweet and sour sauce.
  7. In a bowl, add in 1 tbsp of corn starch and 2 tbsp of water and mix well.
  8. Add the thickner to your sauce slowly and continue to stir. (if you'd like your sauce more thick add more corn starch)
  9. Thouroughly stir, pour into a serving cup, and serve.
